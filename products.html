TIMBER

Timber has an evident existence in interiors since ages. It is one kind of product that can’t be replaced. Each and every log of timber that is cut has a different grain pattern which is unique in its own way.

TEAK WOOD

Timber is broadly divided into two categories – teak and non-teak. The teak species of timber are the once which are deciduous trees and are generally Hardwood. This wood contains higher content of oil, are brownish in color, more stable and durable in nature.

NON-TEAK WOOD

They are derived from gymnosperm trees. They are generally lighter in color. Species like Pinewood, Oakwood, Beechwood, American Walnut, etc. are examples of stable non-teak woods.

BURMA TEAK WOOD

The teak wood that is cut from the forests of Burma is considered the finest grade of Teak wood. It is widely used for application in premium residential and commercial projects. It is available in various sections.

INDIAN TEAK WOOD

The teak wood found in India is one of the best quality woods. It has one of the highest amount of oil content which makes it the more durable and stable wood species. The appearance of wood has a touch of dark brown grains.

AFRICAN TEAK WOOD

The teak which is imported from Africa is economical and affordable. The wood quality is considerably fair. The popular teak wood species from Africa are Ghana, Nigeria, Sudan, Etc.

SALWOOD

Salwood is quite durable and a cheaper semi-hardwood timber widely used in commercial applications. It comes with tough and coarse grains.

OAK WOOD

Oak wood is one of the most versatile hardwood. It is strong, stable and can be easily stained with any kind of paint, varnish, finish, etc. Oak wood is close-grained and is lighter in color.

WALNUT WOOD

Walnut wood is one the most durable and stable hardwood. Walnut is fine grained and is dense in nature. It is widely used in interior décor and furniture making in western countries.

BEECH WOOD

Beech wood is from deciduous trees. It is medium weighted soft timber. It has close straight grains, provides good finishing after polish which makes it ideal for craftmanship. It is commonly used in European countries.

PINEWOOD

Pinewood is a Non-Teak wood species. It is cut from coniferous trees. Pinewood is one of the most extensively used lumber in the western countries. It is a yellow wood and more suitable for cold climates.
