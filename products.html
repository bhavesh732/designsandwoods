TEAK WOOD

Timber is broadly divided into two categories – teak and non-teak. The teak species of timber are the once which are deciduous trees and are generally Hardwood. This wood contains higher content of oil thus are browner and durable in nature.

NON-TEAK WOOD

The Non-Teak species of wood are considered to be softwood. They are derived from gymnosperm trees. Softwood are not necessarily softer than hard. They are generally lighter in color and possess low oil content.

BURMESE TEAK WOOD

Teak wood if found in various countries around the world. The best quality of brown teak is from Burma. Burma teak is available in almost all sizes. This wood is widely used for premium applications.

INDIAN TEAK WOOD

The teak found in the republic of India is the heaviest wood. It has the highest amount of oil content in it which makes it the most durable wood species. There sawn sizes are darker in shade. Due to high quantity of oil the shade of wood has a touch of dark brown grains.

AFRICAN TEAK WOOD

The affordable teak is provided by Africa. The teak which is imported from Africa is economical. The wood quality is considerably fair as major teak that is cut are from commercial plantations. The teak popular from African countries are Ghana, Nigeria, Sudan, Etc.

PLYWOOD

The modern plywood is a mixture of the aesthetics and the functionality. The overlapping of layers of wood makes plywood suitable for making of furniture. Plywood is available in three major grades – MR(moisture resistant) grade plywood, BWR(boiling water resistant) grade plywood and Marine grade plywood.

VENEERS

Veneers are modern day innovation for providing the aesthetics of wood in our interior at an affordable price. They are used where premium finishes are required and where touch and feel are critical to user like in executive office, home office, reception desk, etc.

LAMINATES

Laminates are the most commonly used finishes for furniture. There are various types of laminates such as regular, matt, gloss, decorative, textured and so on. They are scratch resistant and easy to clean.

SALWOOD

Salwood is durable and is a demanded for commercial applications. It comes with tough and coarse grains. It is very light in appearance and can turn into brown color with prolong exposure to sunlight.

WALNUT WOOD

Walnut wood is warm and rich in color. Walnut is fine grained and is dense in nature. It polishes to very smooth finishing, making it idea for furniture making and carving.

OAK WOOD

Oak wood is the most versatile wood. It is strong, stable and can be easily stained with any kind of paint, varnish, finish, etc. Oak wood is close-grained and is lighter in color.

PINEWOOD

Pinewood is a Non-Teak wood species. It is cut from coniferous trees. Pinewood is one of the most extensively used lumber in the western countries. It is a yellow wood and more suitable for cold climates.

BEECH WOOD

Beech wood is from deciduous trees. It is medium weighted timber. Beechwood is hard and tough. It has close straight grains and has to good finishing after polish. It is durable to wear and tear.
